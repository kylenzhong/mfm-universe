## TL;DR

- **The single most interesting movement: Apple made the AI model a Settings toggle.** At WWDC this morning (Tim Cook's last keynote as CEO), Apple shipped a Gemini-powered Siri — a custom 1.2T-param model licensed from Google at ~$1B/yr — *and* an iOS 27 "Extensions" system that lets users pick ChatGPT, Gemini, or **Claude** as the model behind Apple Intelligence. Gemini is default; Claude is now a native iPhone option for the first time, across ~2.2B devices. The strategic read is unambiguous: when the world's most valuable company decides the frontier model is a rentable, swappable slot, the model layer is officially a commodity. What doesn't swap when you change the toggle is your accumulated personal context — and that is the only thing left worth owning.
- **Consumer-side it's a two-event week, both about attention, not apps.** WWDC today (Claude/Gemini/ChatGPT now consumer-OS-level choices) and the **SpaceX IPO** — prices Thu June 11, trades Fri June 12 as SPCX at a ~$1.75T target with **30% of the float routed to retail** through Robinhood/Fidelity/Schwab (3x normal). The leaderboard is reshuffling monthly and Apple just added a 2B-device wildcard. Picking a horse is a trap; building on the layer *between* the horses is the play.

## Top 3 buildable opportunities

The board got re-pointed by the news again, not by fresh quant — and one standing opp got demoted because its forcing function legally evaporated (see opp 3 + kill list). Honest re-rank.

### 1. "Own your AI memory" — a portable, auditable memory/context export layer

- **What / stage** — a tool that pulls a user's accumulated memory/profile out of ChatGPT (and Claude, Gemini, Character.AI) into a portable, human-readable `memory.json` the user controls, with a one-screen "here's what the AI silently decided to keep about you" audit view. Consumer AI / data-portability. Live category, getting more live by the week.
- **Velocity / cross-validation** — four independent vectors, three of them fresh today: (a) **WWDC** makes the model a swappable slot, so the only stickiness is portable context — Apple even ships "personal context access" as a Siri feature; (b) the **CDT report out today** documents **37 manipulative dark patterns** across ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini/Replika/Character.AI, naming "friction asymmetry — easy to sign up, hard to delete, hard to see what's retained" and "emotional-dependency cultivation" as categories — that is a regulator-facing artifact describing exactly the problem this tool solves; (c) **EU AI Act** transparency obligations hit **Aug 2 — 55 days**; (d) last week's ChatGPT Dreaming V3 made profiling automatic + free-tier (96% of memories created unilaterally, per the Feb arXiv study).
- **Hypothesis** — as the model becomes a toggle and memory goes automatic/invisible, the felt problem flips from "make the AI remember me" to "show me — and let me move — what it remembers, before I switch models." No incumbent ships a clean export button; it's anti-retention for them, and the CDT report just made the absence of one a named compliance liability.
- **MVP** — static page + thin serverless fn: ingest a ChatGPT/Claude memory export (both are already user-exportable in settings), parse + categorize + flag the unilateral/stale entries against the CDT taxonomy, emit a portable `memory.json`, offer "rehydrate into a Claude project." ≤1 week. Measure 72h signups off one r/OpenAI + one r/privacy thread.
- **Why now** — the keynote that made the model a commodity ran this morning; the report that named the deletion-friction problem published this morning; the EU clock starts in 55 days. First mover defines "AI memory portability" before incumbents bolt on a deliberately-ugly export flow.
- **Confidence: 4/5** *(the standing off-ramp thesis, re-based onto today's strongest premise yet: Apple proved the model is the commodity).*

### 2. "Bring-your-context" SDK for agent builders — the B2B twin

- **What / stage** — the same memory-export primitive, packaged for builders: a small SDK + open `memory.json` schema so any agentic app lets a user land with their context pre-loaded ("Sign in with your memory") instead of cold-starting. Builder infra.
- **Velocity / cross-validation** — WWDC is the proof point: Apple's Extensions system is, functionally, a consumer-facing admission that the model is interchangeable and the *context* is what should follow the user. With Grok 4.3 at $0.50/$2, Gemini 3.5 Flash at $1.50/$9, and Claude Opus 4.8 Fast Mode ~3x cheaper, models are interchangeable on price too — so switching cost is now *only* context. MCP is foundation-donated and OpenAI adopted Agent Skills, so the standards layer consolidated; the **user-context portability layer is still empty.**
- **Hypothesis** — when models are commodities (Apple just said so out loud), the thing that retains a user is accumulated context. A neutral portability standard for that context is the high-value, un-owned layer. Consumer wedge (opp 1) feeds the B2B standard.
- **MVP** — publish opp 1's `memory.json` as an open spec + a ~50-line import helper; get one indie agent app to adopt "Sign in with your memory." Measure: one external adopter in 2 weeks.
- **Why now** — the standards layer just consolidated and Apple just validated the thesis on the biggest stage there is; the context layer is the next obvious one and currently nobody owns it.
- **Confidence: 4/5** *(bumped from 3 — WWDC is direct, public validation of the premise).*

### 3. Dark-pattern & disclosure compliance scanner for consumer AI apps — re-based off the dead Colorado clock

- **What / stage** — drop-in middleware + a hosted scanner for companion/character/consumer AI apps: disclosure ("not a licensed professional"), crisis-language detection + hand-off, deletion/export-friction audit, and a CDT-taxonomy dark-pattern report. Regtech / builder infra.
- **Velocity / cross-validation** — the forcing function changed this week and you have to underwrite the new one: **the Colorado AI Act's June 30 effective date was pushed to Jan 1, 2027 (SB 189, signed May 14; enforcement stayed Apr 27; DOJ + xAI suing).** Note the conflict — some news aggregators are still printing "June 30, 22 days"; the legal-firm trackers (Hunton, MoFo, Brownstein) say delayed. Trust the statute. So the near-term clock is now **EU AI Act Aug 2 (55 days)** plus today's **CDT 37-dark-pattern report**, plus companion-specific state bills still advancing (Colorado HB 1263 chatbot-safety passed the legislature, awaiting signature; PA's Character.AI fake-medical-license suit in discovery; FL's OpenAI suit).
- **Hypothesis** — the patchwork of companion-liability enforcement is real and widening, but the single biggest, dated, unavoidable forcing function is now Brussels, not Denver. A scanner that maps an app against the EU transparency requirements + the CDT taxonomy wins on time-to-compliance.
- **MVP** — npm package `disclosure()` / `crisisDetect()` + a hosted endpoint that scans an app's flows and emits a CDT-mapped dark-pattern report. Free <10K MAU.
- **Confidence: 3/5** *(downgraded from the prior brief's framing — the Colorado deadline that anchored it is gone; the thesis survives only re-based onto EU Aug 2 + the CDT report).*
